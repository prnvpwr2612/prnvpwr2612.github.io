About Me

I'm a PhD candidate in Computer Science at Stanford, focusing on machine learning interpretability. My research explores how we can build AI systems that are both powerful and transparent, enabling better decision-making in high-stakes domains like healthcare and climate science.

My current work centers on explainable AI (XAI), specifically developing visualization techniques for deep neural networks. I'm particularly interested in applications in medical imaging and climate modeling, where understanding model decisions is crucial for real-world deployment.

I believe in the power of interdisciplinary research. By collaborating with domain experts, we can create AI systems that truly solve real-world problems. My approach combines rigorous theoretical foundations with practical applications that make a tangible impact.

Feel free to explore my publications, projects, and blog posts to learn more about my work.
